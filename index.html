Sound familiar?

You've bookmarked the AI tools. You've tried a few. So why are you still going in circles?

"I use AI daily. But I couldn't tell you if it's actually moving my business forward or just making me feel productive."

You're putting in the time. But busy isn't the same as progress. Without a clear framework, AI becomes another task on your list instead of the thing that clears your list.

"Every week there's a new AI tool someone swears by. I try it, move on, and somehow still feel behind."

Trying new tools isn't the problem. Not knowing which ones are actually relevant to your business is. Without that filter, every new tool just adds to the noise.

"Every networking event, every social media scroll, someone's talking about a new AI tool. I'm tired of hearing about it but scared to ignore it."

That tension is real. And it's exhausting. The answer isn't to keep up with everything. It's to know exactly what matters for where you are right now.

"I keep saving AI tools to 'look at later.' By the time later comes, I don't even remember why I saved it or how it applies to my business."

Saving things feels like progress. It isn't. What you need isn't more resources. You need to know what to do first.

What we'll cover

Five things. 60 minutes. You leave with a plan.

No generic AI tips. No slides full of theory. Just the thinking and the one live exercise that changes how you approach this from tonight forward.

01
The 20-hour rule, and why tonight is your first real hour.

It takes 20 hours to get proficient at any new skill. Most people have been delaying the first hour for years. We start the clock tonight.

02
Stop thinking about who to hire. Start thinking about what actually needs doing.

Stop asking "who do I need?" Start listing what they actually do. Then decide what AI can handle. This one shift changes how you see every role in your business or job.

03
Live exercise: break one real task down and find exactly where AI fits.

You pick something from your own work. You map it. You leave with one specific thing to automate.

04
AI gives bad output because nobody trained it. Here's how to fix that.

You train AI the same way you train a new employee. Clear instructions. Real examples. Feedback. Most people skip all three. We fix that tonight.

05
Where this is all heading, and how to position yourself ahead of it.

The future is individuals who bring their own AI systems to the table. We'll show you what that looks like and what your first move is.
